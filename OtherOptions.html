Downsides to the Alternatives of Nuclear Energy

Coal

Burning coal releases a large amount of pollutants, such as CO2, SO2, NOx, and other harmful things, into the atmosphere

Coal power plants cause approximately 100,000 human deaths per 1000 terrawatt-hours(TWh)

Petroleum

Also releases many pollutants such as CO and NOx which harm the atmosphere and life within

High risk of oil spills, which are dangerous for marine ecosystems aswell as coastal ecosystems

Natural Gas

Releases methane, a potent greenhouse gas, into the atmosphere

Fracking to extract Natural Gas can induce earthquakes aswell as contaminate nearby groundwater

Solar

Unreliable energy source, as dependence on sunlight means intermittency

Uses many minerals and materials which are expensive and rare, with finite lifespans

Wind

Dangerous to wildlife, as flying creatures such as insects, birds, and bats often collide with the large turbines and die

Inconsistent power output, as wind itself is inconsistent in nature

Hydroelectric

Dams disrupt local ecosystems, causing fish and other water-dependent wildlife to alter their behavior to compensate

High maintenence due to sediments and constant pressure on the dams themselves

Geothermal

Fracking for extraction can induce earthquakes

Limited use-cases, only available in regions with high geothermal activity, such as a volcano

Biofuel

Requires mass farming and deforestation, which is harmful to wildlife and our food supply

Release of greenhouse gases due to mass deforestation and improper agricultual practices
