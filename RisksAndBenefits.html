Risks and Benefits of Nuclear Energy

Risks

Catastrophic meltdowns due to human error while operating and maintaining reactors

High initial costs, ranging from 5-10 billion dollars on average

Mismanagement of waste, due to human error and/or negligence

Considered to be non-renewable because of its dependence on Uranium from the earth to generate power.

Chernobyl, Ukraine

Benefits

Very few human casualties compared to competing options, at only approximately 90 deaths per 1000 terrawatt-hours(TWh).

Provides consistent base power loads, which keep the power grid stable and healthy

Long average lifespan, and while originally estimated to be 40 years, the lifespan of most reactors is up to 80+ years.

Low climate risks, with significantly lower pollution and greenhouse gas emissions than most other energy sources.

Lots of room to grow, as research on the improvement and better utilization of nuclear energy is far from its fullest potential.

Small land footprint, as nuclear power plants are not highly dependant on location and take up much less space than its close alternatives.
