How Does Nuclear Energy Work?

Nuclear Energy, at its core, is steam powered.

What this means is that when the evaporated water flows through a chamber which contains a turbine, the turbine is spun and energy is created.

This characteristic is shared with multiple different energy solutions..

The way the water is heated is what sets them apart.

The water within the reactor is heated by a process called Nuclear Fission

This process causes the nucleus of an atom of Uranium-235 to be split by bombarding it with neutrons and protons.

This causes a controlled chain reaction, which creates large amounts of heat, therefore heating the water within the reactor.

The Uranium-235(U-235 for short) is organized in fuel rods, which are made of of small ceramic pellets stacked and sealed into metal tubes.

These rods do not have an infinite lifespan. When the U-235 has been depleted, the rods are lowered deep into the earth and disposed of.

This is also known as Nuclear Waste

While there are many misconceptions about this waste, these rods do no harm hundreds of feet below the surface and contained within their metal enclosures.
